Our Embroidery Heritage

At BlueBird, we preserve centuries-old embroidery techniques while innovating for modern aesthetics. Our artisans spend 20-25 hours on each piece, using only the finest silks, gold threads, and organic fabrics.

What is Embroidery Work ?

Embroidery is like drawing on cloth using a needle and thread. People have been doing this for thousands of years, and we've found examples of it in old places all over the world, like China and Europe. At first, it was just a way to fix clothes and make them stronger. But soon, people started using it to make things look pretty. Fancy embroidery even showed how rich or important someone was in many cultures. From decorating clothes and things for the house to making special religious items and big wall hangings, embroidery has always been a way for people to show who they are and what's important to them.

Masterful Techniques

Zardozi Embroidery

Our signature technique using real gold and silver threads with precious stones. Each piece requires 80-120 hours of meticulous handwork, creating dimensional textures that catch the light beautifully.

Chikankari

Delicate shadow work embroidery originating from Lucknow, India. We use ultra-fine cotton threads on breathable muslin for ethereal white-on-white designs perfect for warm weather elegance.

Sashiko

Japanese-inspired functional embroidery using geometric running stitches. We've adapted this traditional mending technique into contemporary patterns that reinforce fabric while creating striking visual texture.
